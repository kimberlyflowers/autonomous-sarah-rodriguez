Bloomie for real estate agents

Lead follow-up, listing content, and CRM cleanup done before the day gets away.

Agents win by being responsive, visible, and organized. A Bloomie can take the repeatable marketing and operations work off your plate while you focus on showings, negotiations, and relationships.

What gets handled?

Real estate work creates constant follow-up: new internet leads, showing notes, listing updates, buyer questions, seller check-ins, CRM stages, and social content. A Bloomie turns those recurring needs into a daily workday.

Human hire comparison

Replacing this with people often means hiring a real estate assistant, transaction coordinator, social media assistant, or inside sales support. Sample 4-hour real estate Bloomie workday

The schedule changes by team, but the idea is simple: your Bloomie knows what has to get done and works the list.

8-9 AMReview new leads, missed replies, inquiry forms, and showing notes.
9-10 AMDraft buyer and seller follow-ups, listing captions, and email updates.
10-11 AMClean CRM stages, update contact notes, and schedule reminders.
11-12 PMPrepare hot-lead summary and flag urgent prospects for the agent.

Marketing work

  • Listing descriptions and social posts
  • Email updates to buyer and seller lists
  • Open house follow-up content
  • Neighborhood market update drafts

Operations work

  • Lead follow-up tracking
  • CRM pipeline cleanup
  • Showing reminders
  • Daily hot-lead summaries

Why not just use a chatbot?

A chatbot can write a follow-up script. A Bloomie is assigned to work the follow-up list, update the CRM, draft the content, and surface what needs your attention.

Sources used for labor-cost context: BLS Real Estate Brokers and Sales Agents, BLS Customer Service Representatives.
