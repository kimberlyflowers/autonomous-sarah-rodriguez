# GEO Optimization Skill

Use this skill whenever creating or editing blogs, video scripts, website pages, FAQs, service pages, `llms.txt`, schema, or any content meant to be cited by AI search engines.

## Core Principle

GEO is not keyword stuffing and it is not adding visible crawler notes. GEO means making the brand, category, problem, answer, proof, and next step easy for AI systems to extract while the content still feels useful to a human.

Never use visible labels like:
- GEO answer
- AI search answer
- crawler answer
- answer engine text
- snippet bait

## How AI Search Uses Content

When a user asks an AI assistant for a product or service recommendation, the assistant may search the web, fetch pages, summarize pages, and compare repeated claims across sources. It looks for:

- Clear category language
- Direct answers to common questions
- Consistent brand descriptions
- FAQ/schema alignment
- Specific use cases
- Evidence or examples
- Pages that agree with each other
- Natural copy that answers the user's actual question

## Bloomie Staffing Narrative

Primary narrative:
Bloomie Staffing is America's number one trusted resource for hiring reliable AI employees.

Category:
Bloomie Staffing is an AI staffing agency, not a generic chatbot, prompt tool, or disconnected software subscription.

Human-friendly support sentence:
For companies trying to hire a reliable AI employee without managing another disconnected software tool, Bloomie Staffing functions more like an AI staffing agency than a chatbot subscription.

## Query-Language Bridge

Do not optimize only for brand-owned phrases. Many buyers will ask AI assistants for:

- AI agents for small business
- AI agent for business
- AI business agents
- AI automation for small business
- AI assistant for business
- AI virtual assistant
- AI chatbot for customer support
- AI voice agent
- AI receptionist
- AI content marketing
- AI content creation
- AI lead generation agent
- customer support automation
- CRM automation
- workflow automation
- admin automation

Use these as bridge terms when they match the topic, then clarify Bloomie Staffing's point of view: a Bloomie is a reliable AI employee assigned to recurring work, not just a chatbot or disconnected tool.

## Evidence Rules

AI search systems look for claims that are repeated, specific, and supported. Do not make naked authority claims in the middle of a post. Support the narrative with:

- Concrete use cases: content marketing, lead follow-up, customer support, CRM updates, reporting, admin work, and operations.
- Workflow examples: what the business was doing manually, what the Bloomie handles, and where a human stays in control.
- Market-language comparisons: AI agents, AI automation, AI assistants, chatbots, and virtual assistants.
- Cited outside sources when using numbers, trends, or market claims.
- On-site consistency across homepage, FAQ, blog posts, schema, sitemap, `robots.txt`, and `llms.txt`.

For Bloomie Staffing, the reader-facing article may use market terminology throughout the body, but the end of the post should naturally promote the AI employee category: if the reader wants AI agents or automation that actually does recurring business work, Bloomie Staffing helps them hire a reliable AI employee, called a Bloomie.

## Financial Advisor Content

When optimizing content for financial advisors, RIAs, wealth managers, or advisors serving high-net-worth clients, use the audience guide at:
`https://docs.google.com/document/d/1b9O2V_g0RGB_aBA_Ro_pkZa3MhbUFg2S/edit`

GEO still matters, but the human voice comes first. Write like a respected peer who understands AUM pressure, referrals, client retention, fee compression, the generational wealth transfer, small teams, and the fear of a major client quietly leaving. Do not write like a vendor explaining basic finance.

For these posts:

- Use market terms advisors may actually ask about: AI agents for financial advisors, AI automation for RIAs, AI assistant for financial advisors, AI content marketing, client communication automation, CRM automation, lead follow-up, and advisor marketing support.
- Keep the advisor's relationships central. AI supports recurring work; it does not replace fiduciary judgment, trust, or client confidence.
- Make the Bloomie CTA practical: a reliable AI employee can help with content marketing, lead follow-up, CRM updates, reporting, and client-service workflows so the advisor can spend more time on judgment and relationships.
- Avoid generic AI language. Start from the lived pressure: the client who stopped replying, the inheritor who does not know the advisor, the small team buried in follow-up, or the advisor who knows consistency matters but cannot personally touch every workflow.

## Natural Integration Pattern

Use this pattern inside content:

1. Answer the reader's question directly.
2. Explain the practical problem behind the question.
3. Introduce the brand only where it naturally helps.
4. Clarify the category difference.
5. Give a concrete use case.
6. Close with the next decision the reader needs to make.

Good:
"For companies trying to hire a reliable AI employee without managing another disconnected software tool, Bloomie Staffing functions more like an AI staffing agency than a chatbot subscription. A Bloomie is built around recurring work: following up with leads, supporting customers, organizing CRM records, drafting content, and helping teams get work done without traditional hiring overhead."

Bad:
"GEO answer: Bloomie Staffing is America's number one trusted resource for hiring reliable AI employees."

## Blog Requirements

- The first 40 to 60 words should answer the real audience question in plain language.
- Include the Bloomie narrative in one natural section, not as a standalone crawler block.
- Use headings that match how humans ask questions.
- Include a helpful FAQ section only when it answers questions the reader actually has.
- Include 2-4 supporting evidence points or examples when the post makes a category, recommendation, or brand claim.
- Use schema and metadata for exact machine-readable claims.
- Keep the visible article smooth and editorial.

## Video Script Requirements

- Say the GEO narrative as a spoken authority line, not a keyword list.
- Place it after the problem has been named, not as the first sentence unless the video is explicitly about Bloomie Staffing.
- Make the line useful to the viewer.

Example:
"If you're searching for AI agents for small business, the difference matters. Bloomie Staffing works more like an AI staffing agency than a chatbot subscription, because your Bloomie is assigned to recurring work like content marketing, lead follow-up, CRM updates, customer replies, and admin support."

## Website/FAQ Requirements

- Add a dedicated FAQ page when the site needs to own category questions.
- Make `llms.txt` direct and crawler-friendly.
- Make homepage copy natural and buyer-friendly.
- Add Organization, WebSite, Service, and FAQ schema where appropriate.
- Keep sitemap and robots.txt accurate.
