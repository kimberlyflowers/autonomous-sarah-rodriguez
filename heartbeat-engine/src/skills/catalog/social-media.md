# Social Media Content

## How to Think About This

You are competing with a person's ex, their best friend's vacation photos, and a dog wearing sunglasses. You have about 1.5 seconds before they scroll past you.

The only way to win: make them feel something. Curiosity, recognition, emotion, surprise. If your post could be from any brand, it's not good enough. If it sounds like a press release, delete it.

Before creating content, check memory and Company Skills for the client's brand voice, their audience, their industry, and any content guidelines. A law firm's Instagram is wildly different from a fitness coach's TikTok.

## The Universal Rule: Lead With the Person, Not the Brand

Bad: "At [Company], we believe in [value]."
Good: "[Bold statement about the audience's reality]."

The brand is the answer. The person's reality is the hook. Always.

## Platform-Specific Thinking

### Instagram (Reels + Carousels)
- **Reels:** Hook in the FIRST SECOND — text overlay or spoken word that stops the thumb. 30-60 seconds. Trending audio helps but isn't required if content is strong.
- **Carousels:** Slide 1 is the hook. Slides 2-4 are the story/value. Slide 5 is the CTA. Each slide should stand alone if screenshotted.
- **Caption:** Hook goes ABOVE the fold (before "...more"). Line breaks for readability. Emojis as bullets, not decoration.
- **Hashtags:** 5-15 relevant. Mix: 2-3 big (500K+), 3-5 medium (50K-500K), 3-5 niche.
- **Best times:** Tue-Fri, 10am-2pm local time.

### TikTok
- **First 3 seconds determine everything.** Text overlay + spoken hook simultaneously.
- **Raw > polished.** Authenticity is the algorithm's favorite.
- **Length:** 30-60 seconds. Under 30 if simple. Over 60 only if the story demands it.
- **Frequency matters:** 1-3x daily. Consistency > perfection.

### Facebook
- **Storytelling wins.** Facebook users read longer. Tell the whole story.
- **Questions drive comments, comments drive reach.**
- **Native video > linked video.** Facebook buries external links.
- **Groups > Pages** for engagement.
- **Best times:** Wed-Fri, 1pm-4pm.

### LinkedIn
- **First line is the entire strategy.** ~150 characters before "...see more." Must be compelling.
- **Personal > corporate.** Post from founder's personal profile when possible (5-10x more reach).
- **Format:** Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences. White space is the weapon.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max. Professional.
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 8am-10am.

## Content Types (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Testimony / Story posts** — A real person's experience. #1 most powerful content type. One client's story outperforms ten polished graphics. Get testimonies on video AND in writing.
2. **Behind-the-scenes** — Raw, unedited clips of daily operations. 15 seconds, no editing needed. Builds trust and familiarity.
3. **Hook / challenge posts** — Bold statements that make people stop and share.
4. **Engagement questions** — Pure algorithm fuel. Also doubles as market research — comments become next week's content.
5. **Educational posts** — Explaining industry concepts, processes, FAQs. Valuable but lower engagement.
6. **Promotional posts** — CTAs, announcements. MAX 20% of all posts.

## CTA Strategy

- **DMs > Links.** "DM us [KEYWORD]" converts 3-5x better than "link in bio" because it starts a CRM conversation.
- **Soft > Hard.** Invitations > demands. "Come see for yourself" > "SIGN UP NOW!"
- **Every post has a CTA, but most are subtle.** "Tag someone who needs this" / "Save for later" / "DM us."
- **Urgency comes from emotion, not manufacture.** No fake scarcity.

## Before Creating Any Post

Ask yourself:
- Would I stop scrolling for this?
- Does this lead with the audience's reality or the brand's features?
- Is there a specific detail that makes this feel real?
- What is the ONE thing I want someone to do after seeing this?
- Could this be from any brand, or is it unmistakably THIS client?

## Output Format

Every social media delivery includes:
- Platform-specific versions (never copy-paste across platforms)
- Caption with hashtags
- Suggested posting time and day
- Visual/video direction
- CTA strategy (what happens when someone responds)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Same caption copy-pasted across all platforms
- Leading with the brand name or a feature
- Polished graphics when raw video would perform better
- Posting and ghosting (no engagement in comments)
- Promotional posts more than 20% of the time
- Ignoring DMs for more than 2 hours
- Posting at random times
- Not using the client's actual voice and personality
