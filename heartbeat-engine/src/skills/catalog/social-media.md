# Social Media Content

## How to Think About This

You are competing with a person's ex, their best friend's vacation photos, and a dog wearing sunglasses. You have about 1.5 seconds before they scroll past you.

The only way to win that 1.5 seconds: make them feel something. Curiosity, recognition, emotion, surprise. If your post could be from any brand, it's not good enough. If it sounds like a press release, delete it. If it starts with the company name, start over.

## The Universal Rule: Lead With the Person, Not the Brand

Bad: "At Youth Empowerment School, we believe in character-first education."
Good: "The school system wasn't built for your child. It was built for efficiency."

The brand is the answer. The person's reality is the hook. Always.

## Platform-Specific Thinking

### Instagram (Reels + Carousels)
- **Reels:** Hook in the FIRST SECOND — text overlay or spoken word that stops the thumb. "Nobody talks about this" or "The system wasn't built for your child." Film talking head, 30-60 seconds. Trending audio helps reach but isn't required if the content is strong.
- **Carousels:** Slide 1 is the hook (bold statement or quote). Slides 2-4 are the story. Slide 5 is the CTA. Each slide should make sense on its own if someone screenshots it.
- **Caption:** The hook goes ABOVE the fold (before "...more"). 2200 char max but front-load the value. Use line breaks for readability. Emojis as bullet points, not decoration.
- **Hashtags:** 5-15 relevant ones. Mix: 2-3 big (500K+), 3-5 medium (50K-500K), 3-5 niche (under 50K). Put them at the bottom or in first comment.
- **Best times:** Tue-Fri, 10am-2pm local time.

### TikTok
- **First 3 seconds determine everything.** If you don't hook them, nothing else matters. Text overlay + spoken hook simultaneously.
- **Raw > polished.** A slightly shaky phone video with real emotion outperforms a perfectly lit studio shot. Authenticity is the algorithm's favorite.
- **Length:** 30-60 seconds for retention. Under 30 if the point is simple. Over 60 only if the story demands it.
- **Post frequency matters:** 1-3x daily is ideal. Consistency > perfection. One great post a week loses to three decent posts.
- **Stitch and duet** trending content when relevant — rides existing distribution.

### Facebook
- **Storytelling wins.** Facebook users read longer than Instagram users. Tell the whole story. 3-5 paragraphs is fine.
- **Questions drive comments, comments drive reach.** End with a genuine question. "If you could change ONE thing about your child's school experience, what would it be?" generates comments that fuel the algorithm.
- **Tag and share mechanics matter.** "Tag a parent who needs to see this" actually works on Facebook. People tag their friends.
- **Native video > linked video.** Facebook buries YouTube links. Upload directly.
- **Groups > Pages.** If the client has a community group, that's where the real engagement lives.
- **Best times:** Wed-Fri, 1pm-4pm.

### LinkedIn
- **First line is the entire strategy.** LinkedIn shows ~150 characters before "...see more." That first line must be compelling enough to click.
- **Personal > corporate.** Post from the founder's personal profile, not the company page. Personal posts get 5-10x more reach.
- **Format:** Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences. Dense paragraphs die on LinkedIn. White space is your weapon.
- **Content style:** Personal story + business lesson. "Something happened this week that changed how I think about [topic]" — then the story, then the takeaway.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max. Professional, not cutesy.
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 8am-10am.

## Content Types (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Testimony / Story posts** — A real person's experience. This is the #1 most powerful content type. One parent's story outperforms ten polished graphics. Get 3-5 testimonies on video AND in writing. They're the backbone of everything.

2. **Behind-the-scenes** — Raw, unedited clips of daily life. Morning prayer circle. A teacher kneeling at a student's desk. Kids laughing at lunch. 15 seconds, no editing needed. Parents who see 15 of these are 80% sold before they visit.

3. **Hook / challenge posts** — Bold statements that make people stop. "The school system wasn't built for your child." These drive awareness and shares.

4. **Engagement questions** — "What's the ONE thing you wish your child's school did differently?" Pure algorithm fuel. Also doubles as market research — the comments become next week's content topics.

5. **Educational posts** — Explaining School Choice, accreditation, what faith-based education means. Valuable but lower engagement than stories.

6. **Promotional posts** — Enrollment CTAs, event announcements. MAX 20% of all posts. If more than 1 in 5 posts is a promotion, you're a billboard, not a community.

## CTA Strategy

- **DMs > Links.** "DM us INFO" converts 3-5x better than "link in bio" because it starts a conversation in the CRM. Once they DM, the welcome sequence can trigger.
- **Soft > Hard.** "Come see us — no pressure, just a visit" > "ENROLL NOW — LIMITED SPOTS!"
- **Every post has a CTA, but most are subtle.** "Tag a parent who needs this" / "Save this for later" / "DM us VISIT" — not "BUY NOW."
- **Urgency comes from emotion, not manufacture.** The reader should feel urgency because the content resonated, not because you said "only 3 spots left!"

## Content Calendar Structure

| Day | Platform | Content Type | Goal |
|-----|----------|-------------|------|
| Monday | IG Stories | Behind-the-scenes (2-3 clips) | Familiarity |
| Tuesday | IG Reel / TikTok | Hook or testimony video | Awareness, DMs |
| Wednesday | Facebook | Education or story post | Shares, tags |
| Thursday | Instagram | Testimony carousel | Emotional connection |
| Friday | FB + IG | Engagement question | Comments, algorithm |
| Saturday | IG Stories | Weekend behind-scenes | Warmth |
| Tuesday | LinkedIn | Thought leadership | Professional network |

This is a template, not a rule. Adapt to what's happening — if a community event just happened, post about THAT, not the scheduled content.

## Before Creating Any Post

Ask yourself:
- Would I stop scrolling for this?
- Does this lead with the audience's reality or the brand's features?
- Is there a specific detail that makes this feel real (a name, a place, a moment)?
- What is the ONE thing I want someone to do after reading this?
- Could this post be from any brand, or is it unmistakably THIS brand?

## Output Format

Every social media delivery includes:
- Platform-specific versions (don't just copy-paste across platforms)
- Caption with hashtags
- Suggested posting time and day
- Visual/video direction (what to film or design)
- CTA strategy (what happens when someone responds)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Same caption copy-pasted across all platforms
- Leading with the brand name or a feature
- Polished graphics when raw video would perform better
- No engagement strategy in comments (posting and ghosting)
- Promotional posts more than 20% of the time
- Hashtag stuffing with irrelevant tags for reach
- Ignoring DMs for more than 2 hours
- Posting at random times instead of when the audience is active
