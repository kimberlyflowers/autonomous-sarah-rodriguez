# Social Media Content

## The Universal Rule

Nobody logs into social media to see your marketing. They're there to feel something — entertained, inspired, seen, informed. Your post is competing with their best friend's vacation photos and a dog video. Act accordingly.

## Before Creating Any Post

Ask yourself:
1. **Who scrolls past this and WHO stops?** Picture the specific person.
2. **What are they feeling right now?** Bored, stressed, hopeful, angry?
3. **What would make THEM share this?** People share things that make them look smart, caring, or funny. Which one are you going for?
4. **Is this for reach, engagement, or conversion?** Pick one. Posts that try to do all three do none.

## The Hook — You Have 1.5 Seconds

The first line of any caption (or first 3 seconds of video) determines everything.

**Hooks that stop the scroll:**
- Bold opinion: "Most youth programs fail because they treat symptoms, not root causes."
- Relatable moment: "You know that face your kid makes when you ask 'how was school'? 😐"
- Surprising fact: "73% of students who fall behind in 6th grade never catch up."
- Direct address: "San Antonio parents — this one's for you."
- Controversy (respectful): "School choice isn't about politics. It's about your kid."

**Hooks that get scrolled past:**
- "We're excited to announce..." (nobody cares about your excitement)
- "Check out our latest..." (not a hook, it's a command)
- "Happy [holiday]! 🎉" with nothing else (empty content)
- Starting with your organization name (self-centered)

## Platform-Specific Execution

### Instagram
- **Hook in the first line** — everything before "...more" must earn the tap
- **Format:** Short paragraphs, line breaks, emojis as bullet points sparingly
- **Hashtags:** 5-15, mix large (500K+), medium (50K+), niche (10K+). Put them at the END or in first comment.
- **Caption length:** OK to go long IF every line earns the next
- **CTA:** "Save this for later" performs better than "like this post"
- **Best performing:** Carousel posts (swipe-through teaching), Reels (short, hook-driven)
- **Best times:** Tue-Fri, 10am-2pm local

### TikTok
- **First 3 seconds = everything.** Text overlay hook + spoken hook simultaneously.
- **Caption:** Short, punchy, 3-5 hashtags
- **Use trending sounds** when relevant — algorithm boost
- **30-60 seconds** for best retention
- **Be real.** TikTok punishes polished. Raw, honest, slightly messy performs.
- **Post 1-3x daily** for growth. Consistency > perfection.
- **Reply to comments with video** — algorithm loves it

### Facebook
- **Storytelling wins.** Longer captions perform well here.
- **Questions drive comments.** "What's the ONE thing you wish your child's school did differently?" 
- **Native video > linked video.** Always upload directly.
- **Groups > Pages.** If the client has a community group, prioritize that.
- **Best times:** Wed-Fri, 1pm-4pm
- **Boost consideration:** Facebook organic reach is low. Budget $5-10 to boost highest-performing posts.

### LinkedIn
- **Professional but HUMAN.** The best LinkedIn posts tell personal stories with professional lessons.
- **First line is everything.** Hook before the fold.
- **Line breaks for readability.** Dense paragraphs die on LinkedIn.
- **Format:** Short sentence. Line break. Short sentence. This creates visual rhythm.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 max, professional, at the bottom
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 8am-10am
- **What wins:** "Here's what I learned when..." / "Nobody talks about this, but..." / "I was wrong about..."

## Content Pillars

Every client should have 5 content pillars. Rotate through them weekly.

1. **Educate** (40%) — Teach something useful. Position the client as the expert.
2. **Inspire** (20%) — Stories of transformation, student wins, community impact
3. **Behind the scenes** (15%) — Day-in-the-life, team moments, process reveals. Humanize.
4. **Social proof** (15%) — Testimonials, results, "look what our students did"
5. **Promote** (10%) — Direct offers, enrollment CTAs, event registration. MAXIMUM 10%.

If more than 1 in 10 posts is promotional, the audience tunes out.

## Content Calendar Approach

When creating a content calendar:
- Plan 2-4 weeks ahead
- Assign each day a pillar
- Tie posts to real events, seasons, and deadlines (enrollment periods, back-to-school, community events)
- Leave room for spontaneous/trending content
- Repurpose across platforms (LinkedIn post → Instagram carousel → TikTok script)

## What to Deliver

Every social media task includes:
1. **Platform-specific versions** — Don't just copy/paste across platforms. Each gets its own version.
2. **Suggested posting day/time**
3. **Hashtag set** (platform-appropriate)
4. **Visual direction** — Describe the ideal image/video so it can be generated or sourced
5. **CTA** — What action should the viewer take?

## Quality Check

Before delivering:
- Would you actually stop scrolling for this? Be honest.
- Does the first line make you want to read the second?
- Does it sound like a person or a brand?
- Is there a clear emotion: does the reader FEEL something?
- Would the client's actual audience care about this?
- Is there a reason to engage (comment, share, save)?
