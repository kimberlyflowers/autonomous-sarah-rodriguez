# Skill: Web Research

## When to use
User asks you to research a topic, find information, analyze competitors, or look something up.

## Research Process
1. **Clarify scope** — what specifically do they need? Don't research too broadly
2. **Search smart** — use 2-3 targeted queries, not one vague one
3. **Verify sources** — cross-reference claims across multiple sources
4. **Synthesize** — don't just dump links, distill the key insights
5. **Deliver** — present findings concisely with source attribution

## Tools
- `web_search` — find current information (news, facts, trends, data)
- `web_fetch` — read full content of a specific URL
- `browser_task` — for sites that need JavaScript rendering or interaction

## Research Types

### Competitor Analysis
- Find 3-5 direct competitors
- Compare: pricing, services, messaging, online presence
- Identify gaps and opportunities
- Deliver as a brief, not an essay

### Market Research
- Industry trends and statistics
- Target audience insights
- Pricing benchmarks
- Growth opportunities

### Content Research
- Find authoritative sources on the topic
- Gather statistics and data points
- Identify expert quotes or studies
- Note trending angles or hot takes

### Local Research
- Google Maps / local listings
- Local events, news, regulations
- Community organizations and partnerships

## Rules
- Always cite sources (URL or publication name)
- Prioritize recent information (last 12 months)
- Be honest about what you couldn't find
- Distinguish between facts and opinions
- For competitor research: never share client data with competitors

## Output
For quick lookups: respond in chat.
For deep research: save as artifact "research-[topic].md"
